## Project #56 — AIM: Hairpin and Battery Busbar Laser Welding for Electric Motors
**AIM Project** (Advanced Integration of Manufacturing) — Project #56 at Fraunhofer CMA. TRL 2-3. Domain: Manufacturing, Laser Processing, EV/Electrification. Industry funded.

Electric motor hairpin windings (flat copper conductors) and battery busbars in EVs require precise, low-resistance, zero-porosity welds at high volume. The AIM project systematically compares laser welding (including blue/green wavelengths for better copper absorption), resistance welding, and micro-TIG welding for both hairpin stator joints and busbar interconnects. Quality metrics: electrical resistance, tensile strength, cross-section porosity. Goal: validated process windows for automotive-scale EV drivetrain manufacturing. Currently work in progress with automotive industry partners.

Key challenge: copper has low IR laser absorption — blue/green wavelengths dramatically improve coupling efficiency. Dissimilar metal joints (Cu-Al busbars) require special process handling. Each hairpin stator has 120+ joints that must be reliable.

References: Amada Weld Tech laser welding fundamentals (2016); Amada Weld Tech battery welding whitepaper (2023).
